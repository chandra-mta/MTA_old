Grating Motion and Gyro Drift Rate Monitoring

Example: Model Fitting on Roll Gyro Drift

Example: Data - Model of Roll Gyro Drift

On this page, the effect of a grating motion on the stability of the spacecraft are trended. The following steps are taken to produce the trending plots.

  • Extract AOGBIAS1, AOGBIAS2, AOGBIAS3 data around the time when the grating is in motion.
  • Fit a polynomial line (5th degree) on these data.
  • Take the differences between the fitted line and data points of before, during, and after of each grating motion.
  • Compute the standard deviations of before, during, and after the grating motion.

The assumption is that if the standard deviations are significantly different among these three periods, it indicates that the grating motion affects the stability of the spacecraft, and if the sizes of standard deviations change with time, it indicates that the effects of grating motion on the spacecraft changes with time.

Row 1:Standard Deviation during the grating in motion

The red lines are the top and bottom envelopes and the center moving average.
